Zaire's Khichdi Soup Recipe

This is ayuveda recipe that I learned about through an ayuveda recipe book. I think of it as an ayuveda version of chicken noodle soup, since it's great to eat when you're feeling sick.

Photo credit: frugalfeeding.com

Mixing spices for the curry

Photo credit: myrecipes.com

Spices
  • 2 Tbs. ghee
  • 1 Tbs. cumin seeds
  • 1 Tbs. tumeric
  • 1 Tbs. ground corriander
  • 1 Tbs. freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 Tbs. ground cinnamon
  • 1 Tbs. ground cumin
  • 1/2 Tbs. sea salt
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 2 Tbs. minced fresh ginger
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced

Adding spices to the soup

  1. In a large soup pot over medium heat, heat the ghee and add the cumin seeds.
  2. Cook for 1 minute.
  3. Add the spice mix: tumeric, corriander, black pepper, cinnamon, cumin, sea salt, bay leaves, ginger, garlic, and onion.
  4. Cook for 2 to 3 minutes.

Ingredients for the soup

Photo credit: frugalfeeding.com

Soup Ingredients
  • 1 onion diced
  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 1/2 cup of dried split mung beans
  • 1/2 cup of shelled peas
  • 1 cup chopped asparagus
  • 1 cup chopped butternut squash
  • 1 cup kale
  • 6 cups chicken broth
  • 1 can coconut milk
  • 2 sliced chicken breasts

Adding ingredients to the soup

  1. Add the quinoa, mung beans, peas, asparagus, kale, butternut squash, chicken, and broth.
  2. Bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat, add coconut milk, and simmer for 15 to 30 minutes.
  4. Remove the bay leaves and serve.
